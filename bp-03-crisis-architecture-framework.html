Crisis Architecture Framework

A structural framework enabling nations to anticipate, absorb, and adapt to systemic healthcare shocks through architectural, not reactive, mechanisms.

Extended Architecture Overview

The Crisis Architecture Framework establishes the mechanisms a health nation requires to maintain stability under extreme stress: pandemics, cyberattacks, supply chain collapse, mass-casualty events, or political disruption. The framework unifies sensing, decision-making, resource orchestration, and rapid reconfiguration under a cohesive architecture.

Core Structural Elements

  • National Early-Warning Sensing Layer
  • Crisis Intelligence Engine
  • Dynamic Resource Reallocation Protocols
  • Continuity-of-Operations Logic
  • Recovery and Reintegration Algorithms

Dependencies

Upstream: Health Nation OS, Strategic Control Tower
Downstream: Emergency Mobility Network, Cyber-Resilience OS

Questions & Notes
